# Geometric Brownian Motion

In Geometric Brownian Motion, Brownian motion is used to generate small, unpredictable shocks that act on log-returns, not directly on prices. Real asset prices move in percentages—such as "up 1%" or "down 0.5%"—which are multiplicative changes, so modeling randomness in log-price space turns these multiplicative effects into additive ones that are easier to analyze and simulate. Brownian motion provides a mathematically consistent way to model this randomness: over each small time interval, it introduces an independent, normally distributed shock whose magnitude is scaled to the length of the interval, ensuring that uncertainty accumulates correctly over time.

Drift represents the average growth rate of the asset, volatility controls the scale of random fluctuations, and Brownian motion supplies the unpredictable component that reflects new information, market noise, and behavioral effects. These three elements combine additively in log-price space, forming a stochastic process that captures both systematic growth and randomness. Converting this process back into price space by exponentiating guarantees strictly positive prices and naturally produces compounding behavior, so that price changes scale proportionally with the current price level.

This construction also reproduces a key empirical feature of financial markets: uncertainty grows with time. Over short horizons, possible outcomes are relatively concentrated, while over longer horizons they spread out, forming the characteristic "fan-out" observed in real price data. As a result, Geometric Brownian Motion serves as a foundational model in quantitative finance, linking simple assumptions about randomness to realistic price dynamics and providing the basis for option pricing, risk modeling, and Monte Carlo simulation.

Mathematical Summary 

Let W(t) denote Brownian motion. Geometric Brownian Motion models log-price as

    d(log S_t) = (μ − ½σ²) dt + σ dW_t

Integrating and exponentiating yields the price process

    S_t = S_0 exp[(μ − ½σ²)t + σ W_t]

where μ is the drift (average growth), σ is the volatility, and Brownian motion supplies random shocks. This formulation guarantees positive prices, multiplicative dynamics, and uncertainty that grows with time.

Geometric Brownian Motion price paths generated by exponentiating Brownian motion with drift and volatility. The exponential transformation ensures positive prices and produces compounding behavior, causing price paths to fan out over time in a manner consistent with empirical market data.
